Why We Dance

Audry

To say that dance is important to me would be an understatement

My dance journey began when I signed up for belly dance classes at the Momiji Centre near Markham and Kingston Road. I was thrilled to finally find an exercise activity I enjoyed; no more boring step classes for me! After a few sessions the trek to south Scarborough became tedious so I spoke with my teacher and asked if she would consider coming to North Scarborough to teach a class. To my delight, she said she would as long as I found a venue and handled the administration.

Where to start?

At that time I was on the parent council of our neighbourhood school so I felt organizing a class sponsored by the parent council would get more parents involved with the school because once they came to dance together they would surely participate in other school community building events.

The response was overwhelming however the participants were not parents of the children attending the school but residents of the community surrounding the school who were thrilled to have a dance class within walking distance of their homes. Some of the participants had young children which they felt would make it difficult or impossible for them to attend a class in the evening so we arranged for neighbourhood high school students to collect their volunteer hours while supervising school age children. It was a great way to allow parents and children to participate in physical activity since our facility had two adjoining gyms. Our organization evolved as we moved from one neighbourhood school to another, collecting more friends and dancers as we threaded our way through the neighbourhood schools and community centres.

I was never involved with dance before this group; I will never forget our first belly dance performance and the comment my sister made the day before our event. I had stopped to take in what I was about to do; dancing in public for the first time in my life! She said ‘just think of it as jumping out of a ‘plane with the other five people in your group as the parachute!' and that's exactly what happened. I had no time to worry about stage fright, there was so much to do, hair, make-up, jewellery, and of course, remember the steps of the routine! We all landed safely that night and I was hooked – fortunately for me, so was my ‘parachute'! We've had many performances since that night and they have all been empowering, enriching, collaborative, memorable events for which I am forever grateful.

Performance is not mandatory for everyone who joins the dance; some members prefer to learn the routines and would rather not perform. For me, performing challenges me to learn the routine, the exercise benefit is outstanding while rehearsing and I am honoured to work with an incredible group of women to create a unique and exciting experience for all of us.

To say that dance is important to me would be an understatement; for me dance is a form of exercise unlike any other; it's a requirement to have a dance component when I'm exercising; treadmills and traditional aerobic classes are not an option.

According to an article in Psychology Today, "Humans dance because dance is human. Dance is not an accidental or supplemental activity in which humans choose to engage or not. Dance is essential to our survival as human beings." I couldn't agree more!

The article continues: "Any dance tradition or technique represents movement patterns that those persons have found useful for connecting them to something they perceive as having value—whether tribe or tradition, pleasure or skill, community or divinity, heaven or Earth. Dance as movement is inherently relational."

I am always awestruck when I dance with others in the BWT group; it never ceases to amaze me that other people are just as motivated to come to class each week and join the dance. I have had the honour and the privilege to meet so many wonderful people because of my involvement with this group.

We have a routine in our repertoire which we call our ‘Veil Routine'. In this routine, the piece of music to which we dance is "Call of the Wild'; I'm sure everyone would agree we feel deeply connected to this routine and to the music associated with it. I think it's because we are drawn to it and we perform it with a great deal of energy and passion. I think this routine is the embodiment of the name of our group because it brings women together in a powerful, primal energy which is why I dance.

Dimitra

Dance is now a very important part of my life and my health, both mental and physical

From the time I was a little girl, I have always loved to dance. I constantly danced around the house, so at the age of 6 my mother enrolled me in ballet classes at the local community centre. However, I was intimidated by the strict rules and conformity and didn't dance again for another 10 years.

In high school I mustered up my courage and auditioned for the school musical; rediscovering my love of dance and regaining my self confidence in the process. I was so encouraged, that I enrolled at Les Ballets Jazz, a private dance studio in downtown Toronto, and spent another couple of years learning jazz and modern dance. During one of those summers, my mother was in the hospital for several weeks, and I visited her daily, stressed and worried about her and why she was ill. It was then that I also learned about the therapeutic power of dance, as it really helped me to get through that very difficult summer.

Fast forward 20 years and I came across a flyer for belly dance classes, right in my neighbourhood! I hadn't danced since my second year of university, having moved into the highly intellectual world of academia and then full time work. I had left behind my love for dance, much like the discarded toys of childhood. But that flyer woke something inside me. I recalled seeing a belly dance performance when I was about 7 years old Caravan. I was fascinated by it as a child and the opportunity to actually try out this beautiful and exotic dance form was enticing. So with my cousin joining me (it's always easier to try something new and scary with a buddy) I went to my first belly dance class. It was amazing! I loved it! I hadn't realized that I had been neglecting a whole side of myself, having been stuck "in my head" for decades. A side that is so important. I realized that my soul needed this. I NEEDED to dance.

That was in 2008 and I haven't looked back. We recently started adding Bollywood dance to our programme and I discovered a whole new dance style to love. Dance is now a very important part of my life and my health, both mental and physical. It brings me such joy; it enables me to leave my stress at the door for a few hours a week. It gets me out of my highly intellectual mind for a while and puts me in touch with my body. And my soul is much happier now that I am dancing again.

That's why I dance.

Kathryn

Dancing balances things out for me

When I was in Grade 9 I used to walk by the school dance team on my way to the gym and wished I could join them. But I had never taken dance lessons accept Scottish Country Dancing. My parents seemed willing to pay for hockey but I didn't want to do that. I guess I should have been more assertive about what I wanted.

Now that I'm all grown up there is no reason not to dance!

I have several Belly Dance Performances under my belt and I have learned Bollywood choreography. I still do Scottish Country Dancing. I have made life time friends. A dance friend Theresa took this lovely picture of me (above) at a community fund raiser organized by one of our other dancers Vivian. The "executive committee" meet for coffee at our local coffee shop "Athens". We do some business and then we talk and talk. We had a magical night at Nadine's cottage where we Belly Danced in the moon light and experienced a coincidental wolf cry. We also did Yoga. When I was sick and recovering, my Belly Dance friends Audry and Nadine took me away for a nice weekend in Collingwood and they cooked and we watched movies. When I wanted to try MMA class my friend Dimitra was happy to come with me. I miss some lovely ladies who are taking a break like Christine, Kathy, Rita, Rebecca and Tracy. I'm grateful that Corina came back. Thank goodness for Sandra and Dimitra who have amazing timing and memories and let me follow them until I know the choreography myself. And, because I like my sports, I find myself training and racing with and amongst a lot of men. My Belly Dance teacher, Nufisa, pointed out that I have a feminine side. Dancing balances things out for me.

That's why I dance.
